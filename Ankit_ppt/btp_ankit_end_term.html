Automated Solar PV Area Detection and Power Generation Forecasting

Using Satellite Image Processing & Deep Learning

Ankit Choudhary & Apoorva Prashant Jain

Guides: Prof. Ankit Singhal, Prof. Tarun Mangla

Energy Estimation Methodology

  • Core Formula:
    Pac(t) = α × Pdc0 × (Itr(t) / 1000) × [1 + γ × (Tcell(t) − 25°C)]
  • System Efficiency (α): Represents total efficiency from solar generation to AC output (DC-AC conversion, soiling, wiring). We determine α using regression analysis.
  • Inputs: Panel area (Apanel), efficiency (ηSTC), and location coordinates.
  • NASA Solar API: Provides hourly solar irradiance (Itr) and temperature data for any site.
  • Validation: Compared predicted energy with actual site data from WIKA Palwal.

Model L1: Constant Efficiency Assumption

Assumptions

  • Time-Invariant α: The system efficiency factor (α) is constant and does not vary with the time of day or season.
  • Uniform Environmental Losses: Factors like soiling (dust) and shading are assumed to be uniform and averaged into the constant α.

L1 Model: Key Insights

What Worked

  • L1 gives a reasonable first-order estimate of daily and weekly energy.
  • Weekly aggregation reduces random hour-to-hour noise and smooths cloud effects.
  • With a single optimized efficiency factor (α), the model is simple and fast to deploy.

Limitations

  • Systematic bias across hours of the day → mornings and evenings are not well captured.
  • Single α cannot represent geometry (sun angle) and inverter loading behaviour.
  • Daily % errors fluctuate strongly, showing that constant efficiency is not robust at fine time scales.

Takeaway for L3:

L1 validated the physical model and data pipeline, but also proved that efficiency must be time-dependent. This directly motivated the hourly efficiency correction approach in L3.

L3 Model: Hourly Efficiency Correction

From Constant to Hourly α(h)

L1 analysis showed strong time-of-day dependence in the error curve: mornings and evenings behaved very differently from mid-day.

L3 Methodology: we compute a separate efficiency factor α(h) for each hour of the day, absorbing:

  • Sun angle → geometric and incidence losses
  • Inverter part-load behaviour
  • Average soiling and wiring losses per hour band

Result: Hourly mean errors are now centered much closer to zero, with reduced systematic bias.

L3 Analysis: Hourly Time Series

With hourly α(h), the predicted curve follows the actual time series more closely across the day, especially around shoulder hours.

L3 Analysis: Daily Aggregation

At the daily level, L3 reduces both overestimation and underestimation streaks compared to L1, leading to a more stable forecast.

L3 Analysis: Daily % Error

Daily percentage errors are better centered and show reduced variance, indicating that hourly efficiency correction improves robustness to day-to-day variability.

Conclusion & Future Scope

Conclusions

  • Constant Efficiency is insufficient: A single α leads to systematic biases (~34% error).
  • Hourly Correction (L3): Calculating α by hour significantly improves accuracy.
  • Spatial Averaging: Predicting for a cluster cancels out random noise effectively.

Future Scope

  • Weather Forecasting Integration: Incorporate live cloud cover and aerosol data to reduce dynamic errors.
  • Shadow Modeling: Use 3D building data to calculate shadow losses.
  • Deployment: Launch a real-time dashboard for grid operators.
